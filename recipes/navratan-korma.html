Navratan Korma

Description

A luxurious vegetarian Indian curry made with an assortment of vegetables, nuts, dried fruit, cream and spices. The name Navratan means "nine jewels" in Hindi, referring to the traditional 9 ingredients. Served best with basmati rice and naan.

Servings: about 4

Ingredients

  • Spices:
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 1 tbsp coriander
    • 1 tsp turmeric
    • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • 1 tbsp honey
    • salt to taste
  • Curry paste
    • 1 small onion
    • 3 medium tomatoes
    • 1 thumb ginger, peeled
    • 1 green chile
    • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked in hot water
  • Vegetables
    • 1 small cauliflower
    • 2 large potatoes, peeled
    • 1 cup carrots, peeled
    • 1/2 cup green peas
    • 1/2 cup green beans
  • 1 cup paneer, cubed
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins

Steps

  1. Cut all vegetables to the same size. Boil in a pot of water for 7 minutes until soft (not mushy). Drain and set aside.
  2. Puree onion, tomatoes, ginger and green chili. Set aside.
  3. Blend soaked cashews with 1/2 cup warm water and make a smooth paste. Set aside.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Add spices and tomato puree. Cook 7 minutes till thickened, stirring.
  5. Add cashew paste, stir well. Add 1 cup water and cook until thickened.
  6. Once thick, add all vegetables and paneer. Cover with lid on low heat for 15 minutes.
  7. Garnish with almonds and raisins when ready to serve.
